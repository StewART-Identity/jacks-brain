The named parts of a user interface. Front-end work has its own vocabulary, and most of it is just agreeing on what to call each little box, control, and overlay on the screen. Once "the thing that slides in from the side" has a name (a *drawer*) and "the little pill that shows a count" has a name (a *badge*), you can search for it, read its docs, and reach for the right component instead of reinventing it.

Each page below covers one family of elements, defines every term, says when you'd reach for it, and includes a **drawn example of each** so the word attaches to a picture. The examples are inline SVG rendered in the wiki's own colors — sketches of the pattern, not pixel-perfect mockups.

## The six families

- [[upskill/ui-elements/buttons|Buttons & actions]] — the things you click to *do* something: button variants, icon buttons, button groups, and how a button differs from a link.
- [[upskill/ui-elements/form-controls|Form controls]] — where the user types or chooses: text fields, checkboxes, radios, toggles, selects, sliders, and their labels and states.
- [[upskill/ui-elements/navigation|Navigation]] — moving around the app: navbars, tabs, breadcrumbs, pagination, sidebars, and menus.
- [[upskill/ui-elements/containers|Containers & overlays]] — boxes that group or float content: cards, modals, accordions, drawers, and popovers.
- [[upskill/ui-elements/feedback|Feedback & status]] — telling the user what happened or what's going on: alerts, toasts, tooltips, badges, progress bars, spinners, and skeletons.
- [[upskill/ui-elements/data-display|Data display]] — showing structured information: tables, lists, chips, avatars, trees, and stat tiles.

## How to use this

Skim each page once to attach names to shapes — the value is recognition, not memorization. When you later read a component library's docs ([[upskill/web-styling/index|CSS]] underpins all of them — Material, shadcn/ui, Radix, Bootstrap) or a design handoff in Figma, the vocabulary will already be familiar, and the docs will mostly be telling you *which props* a thing you already recognize takes.
