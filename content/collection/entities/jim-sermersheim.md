Jim Sermersheim is an engineer at [[collection/entities/novell|Novell, Inc.]] (1800 South Novell Place, Provo, Utah) and the editor of [[collection/sources/2026-05-02-rfc4511-txt|RFC 4511]], the 2006 Standards Track RFC that defines the protocol elements and wire encoding of [[collection/concepts/ldap|LDAP]].

## RFC Authorship

| RFC | Title | Role |
|---|---|---|
| [[collection/sources/2026-05-02-rfc4511-txt\|RFC 4511 (2006)]] | LDAP: The Protocol | Editor |

His email as listed in RFC 4511: `jimse@novell.com`. He also served as the IANA contact for the LDAP Object Identifier registration that identifies the ASN.1 module defined in RFC 4511 (OID 18 in the LDAP arc).

## Role in LDAPv3 Standardization

Sermersheim edited RFC 4511 as part of the IETF LDAPBIS Working Group, the body that produced the 2006 RFC 4510 revision series replacing the original December 1997 LDAPv3 RFCs (2251–2256). [[collection/entities/kurt-zeilenga|Kurt Zeilenga]], [[collection/entities/steven-legg|Steven Legg]], and Hallvard Furuseth are credited as significant contributors of technical review and content.

Novell's contribution to the 2006 RFC 4510 series was substantial: Sermersheim edited the core protocol document (RFC 4511) while [[collection/entities/roger-harrison|Roger Harrison]] (also Novell) edited the authentication and security document ([[collection/sources/2026-05-02-rfc4513-txt|RFC 4513]]). This reflects Novell's deep operational investment in LDAP — its eDirectory product is a full-featured LDAP server with roots in the X.500-derived Novell Directory Services (NDS).
