Andy Coulbeck was an engineer affiliated with Isode Inc. (9390 Research Blvd Suite 305, Austin, TX 78759; email: `A.Coulbeck@isode.com`) at the time of the December 1997 [[collection/concepts/ldap|LDAPv3]] RFC publications. He is listed as the second author on [[collection/sources/2026-05-02-rfc2252-txt|RFC 2252]] — *LDAPv3 Attribute Syntax Definitions* — alongside [[collection/entities/mark-wahl|Mark Wahl]], [[collection/entities/tim-howes|Tim Howes]], and [[collection/entities/steve-kille|Steve Kille]]. RFC 2252 is the only RFC in the 1997 LDAPv3 cluster on which Coulbeck appears.

Coulbeck was affiliated with Isode Inc., the US entity of [[collection/entities/isode-ltd|Isode Ltd.]] — the UK-based messaging and directory software company also represented in the 1997 RFC cluster by Steve Kille (listed under "Isode Limited"). The Austin, TX address for Isode Inc. is distinct from Isode Limited's Richmond, Surrey (UK) address, reflecting Isode's transatlantic presence at the time.

RFC 2252 defined the formal BNF grammars for [[collection/concepts/ldap-syntaxes|attribute syntaxes]], [[collection/concepts/ldap-matching-rules|matching rules]], attribute type descriptions, and [[collection/concepts/ldap-object-classes|object class]] descriptions used by LDAPv3. In 2006, this content was split between RFC 4512 (information model) and [[collection/sources/2026-05-02-rfc4517-txt|RFC 4517]] (syntaxes and matching rules) as part of the [[collection/synthesis/ldap-technical-specification-architecture|RFC 4510 reorganization]].
