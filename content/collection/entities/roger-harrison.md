Roger Harrison was an engineer at [[collection/entities/novell|Novell, Inc.]] (1800 S. Novell Place, Provo, UT) and the editor of [[collection/sources/2026-05-02-rfc4513-txt|RFC 4513]] (June 2006), which standardized [[collection/concepts/ldap|LDAP]]'s authentication methods and security mechanisms as part of the RFC 4510 series.

RFC 4513 was a product of the IETF LDAP Revision (LDAPBIS) working group. It consolidated material originally scattered across RFC 2251, RFC 2829, and RFC 2830 — the latter two being products of the LDAP Extensions (LDAPEXT) Working Group.

Harrison's authorship reflects Novell's deep commercial investment in directory services technology. Novell's eDirectory (formerly Novell Directory Services/NDS) was a major LDAP server that competed directly with [[collection/entities/microsoft|Microsoft]]'s Active Directory. Novell had strong incentives to ensure the LDAP authentication standards were rigorous and interoperable — weak or ambiguous authentication standards would undermine eDirectory's enterprise positioning.

Email as listed in the RFC: `roger_harrison@novell.com`
