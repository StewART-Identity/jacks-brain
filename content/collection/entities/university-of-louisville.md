The University of Louisville (UofL) is [[collection/entities/jack-stewart]]'s alma mater and earliest long-term employer. Jack spent nearly 18 years there (October 1994–February 2012), progressing from a Student Computer Labs team leader to Technical Lead of the Enterprise Development Team.

## Notable IAM Work at UofL

- **First comprehensive identity management system** (2002–2005): Led technical implementation using [[collection/entities/netiq-identity-manager]] (then Novell DirXML 1.1a); wrote multiple connectors in XSLT — this was Jack's entry point into production IAM engineering.
- **Oracle Identity Manager migration** (2010–2012): Led the full technical migration to OIM; installed and configured database and application infrastructure; developed connectors for [[collection/concepts/active-directory]], Exchange, and Outlook Live.

UofL represents the origin of Jack's IAM career. The Directory Services leadership role and the Novell DirXML implementation established the metadirectory-centric model — authoritative source driving downstream targets via connectors — that recurs throughout his career.
