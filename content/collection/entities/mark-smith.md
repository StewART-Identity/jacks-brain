Mark Smith is a software engineer and IETF contributor who served as editor of [[collection/sources/2026-05-02-rfc4515-txt|RFC 4515]] (June 2006), the revised LDAP search filter string syntax specification. At the time of publication he was affiliated with Pearl Crescent, LLC in Saline, MI. He co-authored the document with [[collection/entities/tim-howes|Tim Howes]], who had originally authored [[collection/sources/2026-05-02-rfc2254-txt|RFC 2254]] in 1997.

RFC 4515 is part of the RFC 4510 series — the June 2006 effort that revised and replaced the original 1997 [[collection/concepts/ldap|LDAP]] technical specifications. Smith's revision clarified that the filter string representation is UTF-8-encoded Unicode, introduced formal `valueencoding` ABNF productions to reduce reliance on descriptive text, and aligned the grammar with companion RFCs 4511, 4512, and 4517. Smith also contributed to RFC 4516 (LDAP URLs), which is the primary consumer of the string filter syntax standardized in RFC 4515.
