The **Directory Implementors' Guide** (DIG) is an ongoing joint publication of [[collection/entities/itu-t|ITU-T]] Study Group 7 and [[collection/entities/iso-iec-jtc1-sc6|ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 6]], maintained by editor [[collection/entities/hoyt-kesterson|Hoyt L. Kesterson II]] as the authoritative compilation of defects and resolutions to the X.500-series recommendations. Version 15 (August 2001) covers the **3rd (1997/1998) and 4th (2000/2001) editions** of the ITU-T X.500-series and the parallel ISO 9594 International Standards.

The Guide is not itself a Recommendation or International Standard, but the Technical Corrigenda (TCs) it reproduces in its appendices *are* formal, approved corrections to the normative directory specifications.

## Scope and Structure

The Guide records defect resolutions in three categories:
- Editorial errors
- Technical errors (omissions, inconsistencies)
- Ambiguities impeding implementation

It does **not** address proposed new features — those follow the normal ITU-T/ISO contribution process through national delegates. This boundary keeps the Guide focused on implementer-reported problems rather than design evolution.

Version 15 is organized as:
- **Appendix A**: Technical Corrigenda to the 3rd edition (1997/1998), per recommendation
- **Appendix B**: Technical Corrigenda to the 4th edition (2000/2001)
- **Appendix C**: Summary table of all defect reports (DR numbers 075–282, with status and disposition)
- **Appendix D**: Defect report form
- **Appendix E**: Defect Resolution Committee member roster
- **Appendix F**: Register of [[collection/concepts/asn1|ASN.1]] module OIDs defined external to the standard

## Most Significant Corrigenda

### Y2K / UTCTime → Time (Defect 211)

A cross-cutting correction applied to X.501, X.511, X.518, X.520, and X.525: all uses of `UTCTime` in operational bindings, security parameters, and chaining arguments were replaced with a `Time ::= CHOICE { utcTime UTCTime, generalizedTime GeneralizedTime }` type. The correction specifies rationalization rules for two-digit year values (00–49 → 2000+, 50–99 → 1900+) and prohibits using UTCTime for dates beyond 2049.

### DIRQOP and GULS Deprecation (Defect 228)

Across X.501, X.511, X.518, X.519, and X.525, all references to the DIRQOP quality-of-protection framework and the Generic Upper Layers Security (GULS) specification (X.830/ISO 11586) were deprecated. The mechanism `OPTIONALLY-PROTECTED` was retained but its companion `DIRQOP`-parameterized variants were commented out in the ASN.1 modules with explicit deprecation warnings. This removed a complex and reportedly broken security framework from the operational specifications.

### X.509 Certification Path Processing (Defect 222)

X.509 TC1 (3rd edition) included an extensive rewrite of §12.4.3, replacing a brief description of certification path validation with a complete formal procedure specifying:
- Input parameters: certification path, trusted public key, initial-policy-set, explicit-policy indicator, policy-mapping-inhibit indicator, inhibit-any-policy indicator
- State variables: authorities-constrained-policy-set table, permitted-subtrees, excluded-subtrees, explicit-policy-indicator, path-depth, pending constraints
- Certificate-by-certificate processing steps covering policy validation, name constraint checking, policy mapping, and intermediate certificate constraints
- Output: success/failure, reason code, user-constrained-policy-set

This is the origin of the structured certification path validation algorithm that appears in RFC 3280 and RFC 5280 for Internet PKI. See [[collection/concepts/x509-pki|X.509 PKI]] for the conceptual context.

### Name Constraints Extension Revision (Defect 273)

X.509 TC3 (3rd edition) and TC1 (4th edition) substantially revised the `nameConstraints` extension to add a `requiredNameForms` component, introducing `NameForms` and `BasicNameForms` ASN.1 types. This allows CAs to require that all subject certificates contain at least one name of a specified form. The object identifier was also changed: `id-ce-nameConstraints` ({id-ce 30}) was replaced by `id-ce-nameConstraint` ({id-ce 30 1}), with 30 added to the set of OIDs not used in the specification.

### Certificate Extension Criticality (Defect 244)

TC2 to the 3rd edition (X.509) clarified that implementations MUST process a recognized extension regardless of its criticality flag — criticality only affects behavior when the extension is *unrecognized*. Prior to this correction, the text could be read as permitting implementations to skip processing non-critical extensions they understood. Specific clarifications were added to keyUsage, extendedKeyUsage, basicConstraints, and nameConstraints subclauses.

### Cross-Certificate Pair Renaming (Defect 257)

The `forward` and `reverse` components of `CertificatePair` were renamed to `issuedByThisCA` and `issuedToThisCA` respectively throughout X.509, with a note that the old terms appeared in previous editions. This clarified the semantics: `issuedByThisCA` holds certificates issued *to* the named CA by other CAs; `issuedToThisCA` holds certificates issued *by* the named CA to other CAs.

### IDP Extension: CA/AA Split in CRLs (Defect 280)

TC3 to the 4th edition rewrote the issuingDistributionPoint extension, adding Boolean components `containsUserAttributeCerts`, `containsAACerts`, and `containsSOAPublicKeyCerts` to address attribute certificate revocation alongside public-key certificate revocation. The rules for which CRL distribution attribute (`certificateRevocationList`, `authorityRevocationList`, `attributeCertificateRevocationList`, etc.) receives each CRL type were precisely specified.

### anyExtendedKeyUsage OID (Defect 275)

The OID `anyExtendedKeyUsage` (2 5 29 37 0) was formally defined, allowing CAs to signal that no extended key usage restriction is imposed without omitting the extension entirely.

## X.501 Corrigenda (Models)

**TC1**: Corrected the definition of superior references (plural vs. singular), fixed the `superiorKnowledge` operational attribute to be multi-valued (`SET OF AccessPoint`), clarified first-level DSA behavior, and applied the UTCTime→Time correction to operational binding validity.

**TC2**: Rewrote the Enhanced Security ASN.1 module (Annex P), deprecating the DIRQOP/GULS framework while retaining `OPTIONALLY-PROTECTED` and `OPTIONALLY-PROTECTED-SEQ`. Corrected `AttributeIntegrityInfo` and `attributeValueIntegrityInfoContext` definitions. Fixed `CONTENT-RULE` information object class (`&structuralClass` typing). Added `CommonResultsSeq` as a SEQUENCE-typed parallel to the SET-typed `CommonResults`.

## X.511 Corrigenda (Abstract Service)

Two TCs corrected alias dereferencing flow charts, security parameter definitions (certificationPath description, time/random descriptions), filter evaluation semantics (three-valued logic: TRUE/FALSE/**UNDEFINED**), SimpleCredentials ASN.1 (ValidityPeriodUTC/GT split), and removal of DIRQOP references.

## X.518 Corrigenda (Distributed Operations)

Corrections to ModifyDN and shadowing interactions, search procedures, APInfo procedure, AccessPointInformation ASN.1 (adding `additionalPoints`), request decomposition constraint, and removal of DIRQOP and GULS references.

## X.519 Corrigenda (Protocol Specifications)

Primarily editorial: ASN.1 module naming corrections (`Realisations` → `Realizations`), removal of `directoryAccessWith2or3seAC` application contexts (GULS/SESE dependencies), and reinstatement of conformance text.

## X.520 Corrigenda (Selected Attribute Types)

Corrections to the `name` attribute type definition, `serialNumber` attribute type (`of a device` → `of an object`), string matching rule descriptions (scope clarified to DirectoryString and its component types), `preferredDeliveryMethod` changed to SINGLE VALUE, `caseIgnoreListMatch` corrected to use `SEQUENCE OF DirectoryString`.

## X.525 Corrigenda (Replication)

Added `subordinates BOOLEAN DEFAULT FALSE` to `UnitOfReplication` to allow shadowing of subordinate entries (not just knowledge references). Corrected access control information handling in shadows. Applied `CommonResults`→`CommonResultsSeq` and DIRQOP removal.

## Defect Resolution Process

Defects are submitted by implementers using a standard form to the appropriate national defect editor. A collaborative **Directory Defect Resolution Committee** composed of representatives from national bodies (Australia, Canada, Denmark, France, Germany, Japan, Norway, Sweden, UK, USA) reviews and agrees on resolutions. Approved resolutions are balloted through ISO/IEC and ITU-T before publication as Technical Corrigenda. The process is non-consulting: submitters receive no individual response.

The Guide tracks defect reports from DR 001 (1st edition, not documented here) through DR 282 (4th edition TC3). Defects 075–175 covering the 2nd edition are documented in Version 14; defects 001–074 covering the 1st edition are in Version 9.
