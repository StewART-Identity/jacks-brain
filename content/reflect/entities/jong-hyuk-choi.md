Jong Hyuk Choi was a researcher at [[reflect/entities/ibm-corporation|IBM Corporation]] and co-author of [[reflect/sources/2026-05-02-rfc4533-txt|RFC 4533]] (June 2006), the Experimental specification defining the [[reflect/concepts/ldap-content-synchronization|LDAP Content Synchronization Operation]] (SyncRepl). His email as listed in the RFC: `jongchoi@us.ibm.com`.

## RFC Authorship

| RFC | Title | Role |
|---|---|---|
| [[reflect/sources/2026-05-02-rfc4533-txt\|RFC 4533 (2006)]] | LDAP Content Synchronization Operation | Co-author (with [[reflect/entities/kurt-zeilenga\|Kurt Zeilenga]]) |

Choi's co-authorship alongside [[reflect/entities/kurt-zeilenga|Kurt Zeilenga]] reflects IBM's interest in LDAP directory synchronization — IBM's directory products, including IBM Tivoli Directory Server, implement [[reflect/concepts/ldap|LDAP]] and would benefit from a standardized synchronization mechanism. The collaboration between [[reflect/entities/openldap-foundation|OpenLDAP Foundation]] (Zeilenga) and IBM (Choi) on RFC 4533 represents a cross-organizational partnership between the two most prominent non-Microsoft LDAP implementers at the time.

## Note on RFC 4533's Experimental Status

RFC 4533 was published as Experimental despite the LDUP working group having adopted RFC 3928 (LCUP) as the standards-track synchronization mechanism. This means the Choi-Zeilenga co-authorship represents an alternative technical direction pursued simultaneously with, and in tension with, the formal standards process. See [[reflect/synthesis/ldap-replication-and-synchronization|LDAP Replication and Synchronization Approaches]] for context.
