Steve Kille is an IETF contributor and software engineer with a long career in directory and messaging standards. His LDAP involvement spans both the LDAPv2 and LDAPv3 eras, during which his organizational affiliation shifted from the ISODE Consortium to Isode Limited.

## LDAPv2 (1995): ISODE Consortium

In the LDAPv1/v2 period, Kille is listed as representing the **ISODE Consortium** (PO Box 505, London SW11 1DX, UK; phone: +44-71-223-4062; email: `S.Kille@isode.com`). He co-authored [[reflect/sources/2026-05-02-rfc1487-txt|RFC 1487]] (July 1993, LDAPv1) — the inaugural LDAP specification — and its successor [[reflect/sources/2026-05-02-rfc1777-txt|RFC 1777]] (March 1995, LDAPv2), alongside [[reflect/entities/wengyik-yeong|Wengyik Yeong]] (PSI Inc.) and [[reflect/entities/tim-howes|Tim Howes]] (University of Michigan). The ISODE Consortium was the not-for-profit consortium that developed the ISODE software suite for OSI/X.500 interoperability; it subsequently reorganized into [[reflect/entities/isode-ltd|Isode Ltd.]]

## LDAPv3 (1997): Isode Limited

By December 1997, Kille is listed as representing **[[reflect/entities/isode-ltd|Isode Ltd.]]** (The Dome, The Square, Richmond, Surrey TW9 1DT, England; phone: +44-181-332-9091; email: `S.Kille@ISODE.COM`). He is co-author on the core LDAPv3 RFCs:

- **RFC 2251** — Lightweight Directory Access Protocol (v3): wire protocol and information model
- **[[reflect/sources/2026-05-02-rfc2252-txt|RFC 2252]]** — LDAPv3 Attribute Syntax Definitions (with [[reflect/entities/andy-coulbeck|A. Coulbeck]], [[reflect/entities/tim-howes|T. Howes]], and [[reflect/entities/mark-wahl|M. Wahl]])
- **[[reflect/sources/2026-05-02-rfc2253-txt|RFC 2253]]** — UTF-8 String Representation of [[reflect/concepts/distinguished-name|Distinguished Names]] (with [[reflect/entities/mark-wahl|M. Wahl]] and [[reflect/entities/tim-howes|T. Howes]])

Kille has a long history in IETF directory and messaging standards predating LDAPv3, reflecting Isode's specialization in messaging and directory software. His involvement in the LDAPv3 cluster placed Isode alongside [[reflect/entities/netscape-communications|Netscape Communications]] as a key institutional contributor to the 1997 specification effort.

The December 1997 RFCs Kille co-authored were later reorganized into the [[reflect/synthesis/ldap-technical-specification-architecture|RFC 4510 series]] (2006): RFC 2251 became [[reflect/sources/2026-05-02-rfc4511-txt|RFC 4511]] and RFC 4512; RFC 2252's content split into RFC 4512 and [[reflect/sources/2026-05-02-rfc4517-txt|RFC 4517]]; and RFC 2253 was superseded by RFC 4514.
