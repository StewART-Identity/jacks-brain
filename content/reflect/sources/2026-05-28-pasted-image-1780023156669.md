A reference infographic classifying all six neuroglial cell types — divided between the **central nervous system** (CNS) and the **peripheral nervous system** (PNS) — with brief descriptions of each cell type's primary roles.

## CNS neuroglial cells

| Cell type | Primary roles |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Astrocytes** | Maintain the blood-brain barrier; metabolic support for neurons |
| **Ependymal cells** | Line the spinal cord and brain ventricles; involved in producing cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) |
| **Oligodendrocytes** | Myelinate CNS axons; provide structural framework for neural tissue |
| **Microglia** | Brain's immune cells; remove dead cells and pathogens via phagocytosis |

## PNS neuroglial cells

| Cell type | Primary roles |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Satellite cells** | Surround neuron cell bodies in ganglia; regulate neurotransmitter levels |
| **Schwann cells** | Myelinate neurons in the PNS; support maintenance and regeneration of neurons after injury |

## Relationship to existing wiki concepts

The distinction between oligodendrocytes (CNS) and Schwann cells (PNS) as the two myelinating glial cell types is foundational for understanding [[reflect/concepts/gray-matter-and-white-matter]] — white matter's characteristic appearance comes specifically from the myelin these cells produce. The ependymal cells connect directly to [[reflect/concepts/central-nervous-system]]'s coverage of CSF production in the brain's ventricles. Microglia's phagocytic immune role distinguishes the CNS from peripheral immunity, where the blood-brain barrier that astrocytes maintain is the first line of defense.
