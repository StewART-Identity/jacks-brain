Architect/Engineer, Identity and Access Management at [[recall/entities/unt-system]].

## Role

Jack Stewart is the primary author and technical lead for [[recall/entities/unt-system]]'s IAM engineering work. He prepared the [[recall/concepts/conditional-access-policy]] and [[recall/concepts/external-authentication-method]] rollout strategy documented in the Entra Authentication Methods Rollout Plan.

## Known Work

- Authored the MFA Reauthentication Frequency & System-Preferred MFA rollout plan (March 2026), covering [[recall/concepts/system-preferred-mfa]] disablement, [[recall/concepts/mfa-sign-in-frequency]] configuration, and [[recall/entities/citrix-horizon]] MFA enforcement via [[recall/entities/microsoft-entra-id]].
- Led the post-migration remediation planning following the January 28, 2026 ADFS → Entra ID migration.
- Authored the [[recall/sources/2026-04-18-2026-04-18-entra-id-multi-tenant-environment-proposal|Entra ID Multi-Tenant Environment Proposal]] (February 2026), requesting two persistent Entra ID P2 tenants (staging and greenfield) to support the [[recall/concepts/iam-testing-methodology]] and avoid repeat production troubleshooting.
- Authored the [[recall/sources/2026-04-18-2026-04-18-authentication-methods-migration-executive-brief-2-|Authentication Methods Migration Executive Brief]] (February 14, 2026), providing University Leadership with industry validation that UNT System's migration challenges were consistent with industry-wide experience, and documenting areas where UNT exceeded typical practice.
- In August 2025, flagged the need to begin the Authentication Methods migration more than a month before Microsoft's September 30, 2025 deprecation deadline. The concern was initially dismissed by colleagues who confused the all-user *Authentication Methods policy migration* with the admin-only *mandatory MFA enforcement for admin portals* — two distinct Microsoft mandates with different scopes, timelines, and user populations.
