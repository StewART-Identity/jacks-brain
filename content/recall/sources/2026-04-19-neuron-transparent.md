## Visual Description

A stylized golden neuron on a fully transparent background, ~512×512 px:

- **Dendrites** — A broad, multi-order branching crown at the top of the cell body, conveying receptivity and complexity.
- **Soma (cell body)** — A luminous golden sphere at the convergence of dendrites and axon origin, with a subtle specular highlight implying depth.
- **Axon** — A segmented, C-shaped axon curving downward and to the right. The segmentation (white gap separating each segment) makes **nodes of Ranvier** visible — a level of anatomical detail absent from the [[recall/sources/2026-04-19-neuron-icon-1024|opaque app icon]] at its 1024×1024 render size.
- **Synaptic terminals** — A small cluster of rounded buttons at the axon terminus, representing signal output.

The transparent background distinguishes this variant from the forest-green–backed app icon and allows the gold figure to be composited onto any surface.

## Observations on Second Read

Comparing across all three neuron assets ingested in this session clarifies the design system:

| Attribute | This file | [[recall/sources/2026-04-19-neuron-icon-1024\|neuron_icon_1024.png]] | [[recall/sources/2026-04-19-neuron-background\|neuron_background.png]] |
|---|---|---|---|
| Background | Transparent | Dark forest-green | Tiled network texture |
| Neuron count | One | One | Many |
| Nodes of Ranvier | Visible | Not visible | Visible |
| Primary use | Overlays, PWA, flexible contexts | App icon / home screen | Site background |

The transparent variant and the background texture share an anatomical register — both show nodes of Ranvier — while the opaque icon trades that detail for visual punch at small sizes. This suggests the transparent PNG is the *master artwork* from which the opaque icon was derived, with detail simplified for the app-store context.
