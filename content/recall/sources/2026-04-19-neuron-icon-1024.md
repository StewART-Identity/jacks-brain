## Visual Description

A stylized golden neuron rendered in a polished, app-store-ready style on a dark forest-green background:

- **Dendrites** — A broad, tree-like crown at the top with multiple branch orders, conveying receptivity and complexity.
- **Soma (cell body)** — A luminous golden sphere at the center where dendrites converge and the axon originates, with a subtle specular highlight suggesting depth.
- **Axon and terminals** — A segmented, serpentine axon curves downward and to the left, terminating in a small cluster of rounded synaptic buttons.

The composition sits inside a rounded-square app icon frame. The gold-on-green contrast gives the icon a premium, jewelry-like quality consistent across all Jack's Brain visual assets.

## Observations on Second Read

Comparing this icon against the [[recall/sources/2026-04-19-neuron-background|neuron background texture]] (ingested the same session) clarifies the design system's two registers:

| Attribute | This file | [[recall/sources/2026-04-19-neuron-background\|neuron_background.png]] |
|---|---|---|
| Neuron count | One | Many (network) |
| Scale | Icon (1024×1024) | Texture (wide-format) |
| Nodes of Ranvier | Not visible | Visible |
| Inter-neuron connections | N/A | Explicit |
| Primary use | App icon / home screen | Site background |

The icon foregrounds the *individual node* (identity, singular); the background foregrounds the *network* (connectivity, plural). Together they bracket the [[recall/concepts/neuron-metaphor|neuron metaphor]]: you are both one neuron and part of a larger mesh.
