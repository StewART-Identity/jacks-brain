Transparent-background variant of the Jack's Brain neuron icon. A ~512×512 PNG illustration of a stylized golden neuron rendered against a fully transparent background, making it suitable for overlays, PWA splash screens, and contexts where the background color is provided by the host surface.

[Download original](/originals/neuron_transparent.png)

## Visual Description

The icon depicts the same anatomically structured neuron as [[recall/sources/2026-04-18-neuron-icon-1024|the opaque app icon variant]], rendered entirely in a warm gold/amber palette:

- **Dendrites** — A broad, branching crown at the top with multiple branch orders, conveying receptivity and complexity.
- **Soma (cell body)** — A luminous golden sphere at the convergence point of the dendrites and axon origin, with a subtle specular highlight suggesting depth.
- **Axon and terminals** — A segmented, serpentine axon curving downward and to the right, terminating in a small cluster of rounded synaptic buttons.

The transparent background distinguishes this variant from the forest-green–backed app icon. The gold neuron reads clearly on both light and dark surfaces.

## Purpose and Context

This variant serves the same [[recall/concepts/neuron-metaphor|neuron metaphor]] as the opaque icon — knowledge is received via dendrites (ingested sources), integrated at the soma (entity and concept pages), and transmitted outward via the axon (wikilinks). The transparent background makes it reusable across surfaces without requiring a specific background color, extending the visual identity's flexibility.
