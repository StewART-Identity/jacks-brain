App icon for Jack's Brain wiki. A 1024×1024 PNG illustration of a stylized golden neuron on a dark forest-green background, rendered in a polished, app-store-ready style.

[Download original](/originals/neuron_icon_1024.png)

## Visual Description

The icon depicts a single neuron with three anatomically recognizable components, all rendered in a warm gold/amber palette:

- **Dendrites** — A broad, branching crown at the top, resembling the spreading canopy of a tree. Multiple branch orders are visible, conveying complexity and receptivity.
- **Soma (cell body)** — A luminous golden sphere at the center where the dendrites converge and the axon originates. Rendered with a subtle highlight to suggest depth.
- **Axon and terminals** — A segmented, serpentine axon curves downward and to the left, terminating in a small cluster of rounded synaptic buttons.

The composition is contained within a rounded-square app icon frame. The contrast between the gold neuron and the deep green background gives the icon a premium, jewelry-like quality.

## Purpose and Context

This icon serves as the visual identity of the [[recall/entities/jack-stewart|Jack Stewart]] knowledge wiki — a personal [[recall/concepts/neuron-metaphor|neuron metaphor]] for how information is received (dendrites), processed (soma), and transmitted onward (axon). The choice of a neuron as the logo directly encodes the wiki's design philosophy: knowledge is networked, not linear.

## Design Observations

- The dendrite structure mirrors the wiki's [[recall/concepts/neuron-metaphor|cross-referencing philosophy]] — many inputs converging on a central idea.
- The axon's segmented, curved form suggests information flowing outward to connected nodes, analogous to wikilinks propagating context across pages.
- The dark green background is consistent with a nature/growth aesthetic, reinforcing the organic metaphor.
